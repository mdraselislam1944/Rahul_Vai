Dumbledore's Army

Also known as the D.A., Dumbledore's Army was an organisation created by Harry Potter, Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley in their fifth year at Hogwarts, intended to teach other students at the school how to defend themselves against the Dark Arts. This secret society became necessary when it was clear that Dolores Umbridge, the fifth year Defence Against the Dark Arts teacher, would not properly teach them anything. The group met in the Room of Requirement and used enchanted coins to communicate with its members. Harry taught the group some of the basics of duelling, such as the Disarming Charm and Impediment Jinx, as well as advanced magic such as the Patronus Charm.

The Potters

The Potter family were an old wizarding family stemming back many decades, but became particularly well-known when Lord Voldemort targeted Lily and James Potter and their son Harry, who was famously responsible for the Dark Lord's downfall. The Potter family could be traced back a twelfth-century wizard, Linfred of Stinchcombe, who made money inventing magical cures and remedies, a feat replicated by Fleamont Potter, whose invention of Sleekeazy's Hair Potion was sold for a vast profit. Linfred's son, Hardwin married into the Peverell family and inherited the Invisibility Cloak - a remarkable artefact that was unlike any other of its kind - and would ultimately make its way down to Harry Potter.

Harry Potter

Harry Potter was prophesied to be the downfall of Lord Voldemort and therefore singled out at birth as the Dark Lord's rival. He was known in the wizarding world as 'The Boy Who Lived' after becoming the only known survivor of the Killing Curse, which was attempted by Voldemort when Harry was just a baby, after both his parents were murdered. But despite causing one of the most famous wizarding world events, Harry did not know he was a wizard until his eleventh birthday, due to his Muggle aunt and uncle neglecting to tell him. He would eventually go on to Hogwarts, discover his magical potential, and embark upon several great adventures with his best friends, Ron and Hermione.

The Invisibility Cloak

One of a kind, the Invisibility Cloak was handed down from generation to generation in Harry Potter's family, starting with the Peverells. Left to Harry by his father, he was unaware of its true origins, or indeed its true powers, and it was used largely in order to travel around the castle and grounds unseen, including trips to Hogsmeade when his lack of a permission slip meant he was banned from the visits. Little did Harry know just how rare an item he had in his possession: this cloak was one of the three Deathly Hallows.

Saving the wizarding world

Harry Potter plays a central role in saving the wizarding world from the dark forces of Lord Voldemort in J.K. Rowling's "Harry Potter" series. Here is an overview of Harry's key contributions to the salvation of the wizarding world. The overarching plot of the series revolves around the conflict between Harry and the dark wizard Lord Voldemort. Harry, as the Chosen One, is destined to defeat Voldemort. Throughout the books, Harry confronts various challenges, faces personal sacrifices, and engages in numerous battles against the dark forces. The ultimate climax occurs in the Battle of Hogwarts, where Harry, with the help of his friends and allies, finally defeats Voldemort, ending his reign of terror.

The Philosopher's Stone (or The Sorcerer's Stone in the US)

Harry discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard and attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. He befriends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, discovers his talent in Quidditch, and learns about the Philosopher's Stone, an object with the power to grant immortality.

The Chamber of Secrets

In his second year at Hogwarts, Harry learns about the Chamber of Secrets, a hidden chamber within the school built by Salazar Slytherin. The chamber is opened, and students are petrified. Harry encounters Tom Riddle's diary, a Horcrux, and eventually defeats a manifestation of Voldemort's younger self.

The Prisoner of Azkaban

Harry learns that Sirius Black, a dangerous prisoner, has escaped Azkaban and is believed to be after him. He discovers the truth about Sirius, the Marauder's Map, and his own connection to his parents' death.

The Goblet of Fire

Harry is unexpectedly entered into the Triwizard Tournament, a dangerous magical competition. He faces various challenges and ultimately discovers Voldemort's return to power. The series takes a darker turn as the wizarding world becomes aware of the looming threat.

The Half-Blood Prince

Dumbledore reveals to Harry crucial information about Voldemort's past and his quest for immortality. The Horcruxes, objects containing pieces of Voldemort's soul, are introduced. Dumbledore is killed by Professor Snape, who is revealed to be a double agent.
